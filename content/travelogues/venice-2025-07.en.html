Venice

Venice is a city built on a lagoon, where canals replace roads. This city was once a powerful maritime republic, with rich art and architecture. Walking through Venice often feels like stepping into a painting.

Santa Maria della Salute

The Grand Canal is Venice's main waterway, and the white dome in the images represents Santa Maria della Salute, a classic and frequently depicted Venetian scene.

Gondoliers On The Grand Canal, Venice (1911) - Federico del Campo

Venetian Loggia

Venice's loggias showcase the architectural beauty of the city, with views of canals and squares visible through the arches.

Memory of Venice, 1918 - Camille Pissarro

St. Mark's Square

This is Venice's most famous square, known as "the most beautiful drawing-room in Europe."

Festa del Redentore, Venice's traditional religious festival held every July to commemorate the end of the plague.
