Verona

Verona is a charming Italian city filled with history and romance. It blends Roman, medieval, and Renaissance influences beautifully. Walking through Verona, you can often see red brick buildings, arches, and narrow streets. The Adige River curves around the city and adds to its calm atmosphere. Overall, Verona feels peaceful yet full of stories.

Juliet's House

Juliet's House is famous because of Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet. In the courtyard, there is a statue of Juliet where many visitors take photos. Although the story is fictional, the place strongly represents love and romance.

Verona Arena

The Verona Arena is one of the best-preserved Roman amphitheaters in the world. Built nearly two thousand years ago, it still looks powerful and impressive today. The arena is still used for opera performances and large events. Standing in front of it, you can clearly feel the scale of ancient Roman architecture. It is the most iconic landmark in Verona.
